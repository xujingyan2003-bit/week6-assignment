Asia

I remember the smell of cumin lamb skewers drifting through the night markets of Xi'an, my dad's hand warm around mine as we navigated the neon-lit crowds. That was the first time I understood that food could tell a story — that a single bite could carry centuries of history.

Growing up, those family road trips across China shaped everything I believe about travel. From the misty karst peaks of Guilin to the buzzing streets of Hong Kong where I now call home, Asia has been my classroom, my comfort, and my constant source of wonder.

Come explore the continent that started it all — one story at a time.

Europe

The first time I stepped off a plane in Europe, I teared up. Not from sadness, but from the sheer weight of standing inside centuries of history I'd only ever read about. I remember sitting in a tiny Parisian café, the smell of fresh croissants curling through the autumn air, thinking: the world is so much bigger than I imagined.

Coming from China, the contrast was staggering — the cobblestone streets, the quiet church bells, the way strangers lingered over wine and conversation. Europe taught me that travel isn't about ticking off landmarks; it's about slowing down long enough to absorb a place.

Dive into my European adventures and see the continent through a traveler from the East.

Africa

Nothing prepares you for the silence of the African savanna at dawn — a silence so vast it hums. I remember standing in the golden grass, watching a herd of elephants move like slow clouds across the horizon, and feeling something shift deep inside me.

Africa taught me humility. In the warmth of a village welcome, in the laughter of children chasing dust, I found a kind of richness that has nothing to do with money. It redefined what "home" means — it's not a place, it's a feeling of belonging.

These stories carry the heartbeat of a continent that changed how I see the world.

Americas

The first time I wandered through a Latin American market, the colors hit me before anything else — towers of tropical fruit, hand-woven textiles in every shade, and music tumbling from every corner. It reminded me of the night markets back home in China, but with a rhythm entirely its own.

Then there was the silence of the North American wilderness — standing at the edge of a canyon so vast it swallowed my voice whole. The Americas taught me that a single continent can hold both the loudest celebrations and the quietest solitude.

Come explore a land of beautiful contrasts through my eyes.

Oceania

There is something about standing on an island at what feels like the edge of the world, surrounded by nothing but endless ocean and sky. In Oceania, I found a stillness I didn't know I was looking for — the kind that comes from being so far from everything familiar.

The ancient stories of the indigenous peoples humbled me, their deep connection to land and sea echoing something I recognized from my own culture's reverence for nature. Oceania reminded me that the quietest places often speak the loudest.

Step into my stories from the most peaceful corner of the earth.

About Me

The story behind the stories.

Hi, I'm Bubbles — a curious soul with a passport stamped with stories from every corner of China.

What started as a childhood spent tagging along on my parents' road trips across China turned into a lifelong obsession. I traded my hometown for a student visa in Hong Kong, but my love for slow travel never faded. Now, as a graduate student navigating the vibrant streets of this city, I still find myself chasing sunsets and writing about the people and places that make this world feel like home.

This blog is my digital journal — part travel guide, part photo diary, part love letter to the places that have shaped me. Whether you're planning your first solo trip or your fiftieth, I hope these stories inspire you to book that ticket, take that detour, and say yes to the unexpected.
